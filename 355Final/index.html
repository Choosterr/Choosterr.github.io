What is Worlds?

Throughout the League of Legends Esports scene, Worlds is the penultimate competitive tournament of the year. This annual tournament brings the best teams from around the world to compete in a multi-staged gauntlet to crown a world champion.

What are Champions?

Champions are the playable characters in League of Legends. Of the 169 champions in the game, teams draft their champions to ban 5 and select 5 for every match. These drafts crucial for success since they give you an in-game advantage through strategic counter-picks or mechanical comfort depending on the player.

What is the Goal?

This exploration aims to identify trends and community myths surrounding the selection of these champions and analyzing some of their performance metrics across years of Worlds competition. From presence rates to flexing across roles, there are many different champion statistics to reveal among the champion data.

Exploration 1: Are Newer Champions Must-Pick?

Since Worlds 2016, there have been 36 new champions added to League of Legends. However, how often do they appear in Worlds following their release? Are they always over-tuned and must be played? This tool aims to filter and visualize newer champions playable during that year of international competition.

Filter by year and role, and highlight champions using a tokenized search box. Click on suggestions to add them, and use the 'x' to remove.
